Agency Partner Model — How should we structure it?

Agencies manage multiple client brands. What pricing model drives the most partner signups?

A

Tiered: Bronze / Silver / Gold

Tier Brands Price/mo Per brand
🥉 Bronze Up to 5 £499 £99.80
🥈 Silver Up to 15 £999 £66.60
🥇 Gold Up to 40 £1,999 £49.98

Agencies pick a tier, get white-label client portals, bulk pricing. They upsell to clients at whatever margin they want.

Pros

  • Predictable MRR per agency
  • Encourages agencies to fill their allocation
  • Clear upgrade path

Cons

  • Agency with 6 brands pays for 15 (Silver) — feels wasteful
  • More complex billing logic
B

Per-Brand: £99/brand/month (agency rate)

Flat discount from £149 retail. Agency pays £99/brand/month, no tiers, no minimums. Volume discount kicks in at thresholds:

Brands Per brand/mo Discount
1–5 £99 33% off retail
6–15 £79 47% off retail
16+ £59 60% off retail

Pros

  • Pay only for what you use — zero waste
  • Low barrier to start (1 brand = £99)
  • Simple to understand

Cons

  • Less predictable revenue per agency
  • No commitment incentive
C

Setup Fee + Reduced Monthly

One-time £199 setup per brand (covers onboarding, keyword research, initial audit). Then £79/brand/month ongoing. Agency keeps the setup fee as margin or passes to client.

Pros

  • Upfront cash — helps fund early growth
  • Qualifies serious agencies (skin in the game)
  • Lower monthly makes it easier to sell to clients

Cons

  • Setup fee is friction for first sale
  • Harder to pitch "free trial" alongside a fee
  • More complex pricing page

💡 Margin note: API cost is ~£20/brand/month. At £59/brand (lowest tier), you still have 66% gross margin. All tiers are profitable.
